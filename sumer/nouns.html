Sumerian Noun Phrases

Sumerian nouns are divided into two groups: animate and inanimate.
Animate nouns are people and gods, and inanimate nouns are everything else (objects, animals, etc.)

Sumerian does not mark gender, so there are no separate pronouns for "he" and "she".

The noun can be followed by adjectives.
lugal means "king" but lugal.gal means " a great king"

Possessive pronouns can be attached to either a noun or a noun+adjective complex
lugal.ani means "his king" and lugal.gal.ani means "his great king"

Sumerian Possessive suffixes
-gu10 my lugal.gu10 "my king"
-zu your (singular) lugal.zu "your king"
-ani his/her (animate) lugal.ani "his/her king"
-bi its (inaminate) lugal.bi "its king"
-me our lugal.me "our king"
-zu.ne(.ne) your (plural) lugal.zu.ne "your (plural) king"
-a.ne.ne their (animate) lugal.a.ne.ne "their king"
-bitheir (inanimate) lugal.bi "their king"

Animate nouns are made plural by adding the suffix .ene, which comes after any adjectives of possessive suffixes
lugal.ene would be "kings", lugal.gal.ene is "great kings"

Both types of nouns can also be made plural by reduplicating the word, which may also give a sense of totality
lugal.lugal would then mean "all the kings" and gud,gud would mean "all the oxen".

After the noun, adjectives, possessive pronouns, and plural marker comes the case ending, which indicates what function the modified noun plays in the sentence. Annoyingly, some of these can often disappear in the texts.

Sumerian Case Markers
Marker Case Meaning Example
.ø absolutive subject of intransitive verb The ball dropped
OR direct object of transitive verb She dropped the ball
.e ergative subject of transitive verb She dropped the ball
.ra dative "to, for" (animate only) She gave the ball to him
.a locative "in" (inanimate only) She did it in the room
.e locative-terminative "to, by" (inanimate only) She brought the ball to the room
.ta ablative-instrumental "from, by" (inanimate only) The ball dropped from her hands
OR She hit the ball with the stick
.da comitative "with" She played catch with him
.še3 terminative "towards" She brought the ball to the house
.ak genitive "of" She lives in the house of her mother
.gin7 equitative "like" The house is big like a mansion
